1 / 3: Stockholm Arlanda Flygplats

Stockholm Arlanda International Airport is the largest and busiest airport in Sweden, and is located twenty-three miles north of the city center in an agricultural and forested area. The Arlanda Airport station has two stops to serve all the terminals. Both stops are below and connect via escalators and elevators to the airport. Commuter service to the north and south as well as direct HSR service to downtown Stockholm are provided.

Due to time constraints, this station was not visited during research travel. Photos from other sources have been used at left.

2 / 3: Oslo Lufthavn

Oslo International Airport is the largest and busiest airport in Norway, and is located about twenty-two miles northeast of the city center in an agricultural and forested area. The Oslo Airport station, also known as Gardermoen Station, is located in a separate structure connected to the airport terminals. The station provides commuter train service, as well as HSR express service direct to downtown Oslo. Similar service is under construction for the city of Tianjin near Beijing to international airport, Tianjin Binhai Airport.

Due to time constraints, this station was not visited during research travel. Photos from other sources have been used at left.

3 / 3: Shanghai Pudong Airport (Maglev)

Shanghai Pudong International Airport is the largest and busiest airport in Shanghai, and primarily serves international travel to the city. The airport is located about nineteen miles east of the city center in a mixed urban/suburban/industrial area. The airport is connected to the city via metro and bus routes, though its most famous connection by far is the maglev, the only train with this particular technology that currently exists in commercial operation. It connects the airport to the Longyang Road metro station, still well outside of the city center. The maglev reaches speeds of up to 268 miles per hour and makes the nineteen mile trip in about eight minutes.
