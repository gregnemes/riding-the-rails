1 / 3: Seoul Station/Yongsan Station/Gwangmyeong Station

High-speed rail development in South Korea (KTX) is relatively new: the first route from the capital city of Seoul to Busan, the country's second largest city and an important east coast port city, was opened in phases from 2004 through 2010. Since then, additional routes have come online with terminal stations in Mokpo, Yeocheon, Jinju, and Pohang.

With the introduction of KTX service in Seoul, several new stations were developed to disperse service throughout the city and suburbs. In the central city area, Yongsan Station was redeveloped to provide service to Seoul for the Honam and Jeolla routes while Seoul Station provides service for the Gyeongbu and Gyeongjeon routes. Both stations are designed as through stations, and select trains on all four KTX routes continue on to Incheon International Airport.

In addition, Gwangmyeong Station (originally called "Namseoul Station" which translates to "Seoul South Station") was developed as a primary suburban station providing service to all four KTX routes. Located about fourteen miles south of Seoul station, the disposition of the station is distinctly suburban. The station offers a large parking facility, as more than half of all passengers arrive to the station by car. Near the station are several large shopping amenities: a Costco, a Lotte Outlet, and the world’s largest IKEA are all within close proximity to the station. For passengers who live in the suburban area south of Seoul, it is more convenient to use this station, rather than travel into the city center to Seoul or Yongsan Stations.

2 / 3: Wuhan Station/Hankou Station

Wuhan is a major conurbation in central China composed of three metropolitan areas: Wuchang, Hankou, and Hanyang. Due to its central location Wuhan is at the intersection of several important freight and passenger rail routes through China: most importantly the Beijing–Guangzhou north-south route and the Shanghai–Wuhan–Chengdu east-west route. Three major HSR stations serve the city, though the Chinese high-speed trains do not typically make multiple stops in a single city: a train bound for Hankou Station will not also stop at Wuhan Station or Wuchang Station. Both Hankou Station and Wuhan Station, however, are served by trains going in all direction, which makes travel to and from Wuhan more convenient than most Chinese cities.

All the stations in Wuhan (and China in general) are enormous. Wuchang and Hankou are designed to respond to a denser urban environment, while Wuhan Station responds to a more suburban* environment. There is a direct connection with nearby highways: exits from the highway actually wrap the upper level of the structure and form an efficient drop-off/pick-up area. Covered parking is incorporated on the ground level under the track approaches. Chinese train stations are usually only accessible to ticketed passengers, though at Wuhan station the ground level is open to pedestrians for strolling and shopping. The entry plazas are more park-like in character, with serene wetland gardens, public art and plenty of bench seating.

*Suburban by Chinese standards.

3 / 3: Istanbul Pendik Station/Istanbul Center

Turkey is the most recent country to develop high-speed rail, and as such the network is far from complete. The urban link from the suburb of Pendik into Istanbul's city center is still under construction, though the lines from Pendik to Ankara and Konya are in operation. Plans show the HSR route connecting Pendik to Söğütlüçeşme Station on the Asian continent, through the new Marmaray Tunnel under the Bosphorous Strait to Sirkeci Station on the European continent, and finally on to the airport and ending at Halkali, a suburban neighborhood northwest of the airport. Further plans include a high-speed route extension to the Bulgarian border at Kapikule.

Istanbul is a city famous for being difficult to move through with any efficiency. Importantly for the city, the development of high-speed rail is concurrent with improvements in metro lines and the development of new commuter lines. When complete, the suburbs around the city will be better connected to the city than ever before, and with the inclusion of the suburban HSR stops at Pendik, Halkali, and the airport, the suburbs will not be dependent on the city center for access to the great region.
