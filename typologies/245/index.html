TYPOLOGY GALLERY: Rural Stations

1 / 3: Gare du Creusot TGV

The Creusot TGV Station, opened in 1981, was the first gare betterave developed in France. Located on the route from Paris to Lyon, the station is situated on a rural site outside of the town of Écuisses and south of Le Creusot. This station location, and even its existence, represents a compromise: DATAR (the French department dedicated to supporting decentralization and territorial development) supported an intermediary station in the area, while SNCF preferred to deviate from the ideal route between Paris and Lyon as little as possible. The resulting station provides regional TGV access to local residents mostly by car, as connections to nearby cities via bus are poor.

Due to time constraints, this station was not visited during research travel. Photos from other sources have been used at left.

2 / 3: Estación de Camp de Tarragona

Camp de Tarragona is an interesting agglomeration of small historic towns, industrial centers, and agrarian land uses outside of the historic town of Tarragona on the Mediterranean coast, about sixty miles west of Barcelona. The city of Tarragona is a tourist destination, well served by commuter rail and express buses even before development of the AVE station seven miles north of the city. Similar to Le Creusot TGV Station, both the dense historic fabric of Tarragona and resistance to deviate from the ideal HSR route between Madrid and Barcelona informed the decision to place the station well north of the city. (The future Barcelona–Valence high-speed route will join the Madrid–Barcelona route at this location.) Given the already good connections with Barcelona and the difficulty of locating the AVE station in the center of Tarragona, the rural station location seems logical in this instance.

Although there is ample room for development in the rural location, the Camp de Tarragona AVE Station is compact with a distinctly urban disposition. Most passengers arrive by car, and the station is legible from the perspective of the driver: parking and pick-up/drop-off are obvious and easy to navigate. A 600+ spot parking garage is situated on two levels below the station, taking advantage of the site’s natural topography. The enclosed area of the station is minimal but bright, with rental car access, a ticket office, a café, and a small shop.

There is no way to predict how passengers will arrive to the station in fifty years time, or how the surrounding territory will develop. Although the location is rural today, the design of Camp de Tarragona station will allow it to be a good urban neighbor if and when development is possible in the immediate area. Unlike Aix TGV, with limited development opportunity due to its position above a highway, or Le Creusot TGV, with its sea of parking holding development away from the station, Camp de Tarragona station seems to be the best resolution of minimal station development with maximum future development potential.
