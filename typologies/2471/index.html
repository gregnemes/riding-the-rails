TYPOLOGY GALLERY: Airport Stations- Within a Network

1 / 10: Paris Charles de Gaulle Aéroport

Charles de Gaulle International Airport, located about twenty-two miles northwest of central Paris, is France’s busiest airport. The surrounding area is developed with compatible uses: hotels, long-term parking structures, industrial facilities, etc. The HSR station is a separate structure sandwiched between two terminals with a drop-off/pick-up area above. The station is connected via four major TGV routes to destinations throughout France, though it is usually more convenient to take the RER (commuter train) when traveling from the airport into Paris.

2 / 10: Lyon St Exupery Aéroport

Lyon–Saint-Exupéry International Airport serves Lyon, France’s third largest city, as well as the entire Rhône-Alpes region in southeast France. The airport lies about thirteen miles east of central Lyon, and the surrounding area is largely suburban. The Saint-Exupéry TGV Station is a separate structure, designed by Santiago Calatrava, connected to the terminal via skywalk. The station is not directly connected to Lyon via a TGV route, though the Rhônexpress light rail connects the station to Lyon Part-Dieu TGV. The Saint-Exupéry TGV Station operates below capacity, though the Ouigo low-cost TGV serves Paris and Marseilles from this station.

3 / 10: Amsterdam Schiphol Luchthaven

Schiphol International Airport is the most important airport in the Netherlands, the fifth-busiest in Europe, and is convenient to many locations in Germany and Belgium as well. It is located about six miles southwest of Amsterdam in a mix of agricultural, industrial, and suburban neighborhoods. The Schiphol Airport HSR station is located below the terminal, accessible by escalator and elevator from the main concourse. The station serves sprinter trains (commuter rail), Intercity trains (HSR), Intercity direct trains (HSR limited-stop), and Thalys international HSR trains.

4 / 10: Frankfurt Flughafen

Frankfurt International Airport is the third-busiest airport in Europe and the most important airport in Germany. It is located about eight miles from central Frankfurt in a largely agricultural and suburban neighborhood. The Frankfurt Airport ICE station is a separate structure connected to the terminals by a long hall. It consists of below-ground platforms, a ground-level skylit atrium with general station services (food, shops, ticket booth, etc), and flanking high-rise developments of hotel and high-end office space called "The Squaire." The station serves only long distance trains, with no connection to S Bahn (commuter rail) services. (The airport has local connections in another location beneath Terminal 1.)

5 / 10: Düsseldorf Flughafen

Düsseldorf International Airport is the primary airport for the Rhine-Ruhr region in Germany, which is one of the largest metropolitan areas of the world. Düsseldorf, Essen, Duisburg, Krefeld, Mülheim an der Ruhr, Neuss, and Wuppertal are important urban and industrial centers all located within twelve miles of the airport. The Düsseldorf Airport Station is a separate structure located to the east of the airport and connected to the terminals via dedicated skytrain link. The station serves S Bahn trains (commuter rail), InterCity trains (HSR), and InterCity Express trains (HSR limited-stop) and is well integrated with the whole of the Rhine-Ruhr region.

6 / 10: Cologne/Bonn Flughafen

The Cologne/Bonn International Airport is centrally located in the metropolitan region, about nine miles southeast of Cologne and ten miles northeast of Bonn. It is located within a nature preserve and adjacent to a suburban neighborhood. The Cologne/Bonn Airport Station is located underground and connected via tunnel to the terminals, though it is lit by a curving glass roof. The station is connected to the HSR network via a ten-mile-long loop, and serves local S Bahn trains (commuter rail), InterCity trains (HSR), and both domestic and international InterCity Express trains (HSR limited-stop). A similar HSR airport loop is under construction for the airport in Stuttgart.

7 / 10: Shanghai Hongqiao Airport

Shanghai Hongqiao International Airport serves mostly domestic flights and is located eight miles west of central Shanghai in a mixed urban/suburban/industrial area. The Hongqiao Airport station is adjacent to the terminals and connected via tunnels. Although designed as a through station, it acts as the terminus of several high-speed rail lines from the north, south, and west. All service is for G and D class inter-province high-speed trains. Connection to Shanghai is provided by the city metro or bus. High-speed trains do not make multiple stops at the other important HSR stations in Shanghai, such as Shanghai Central Station or Shanghai South Station. 8 / 10: Changchun Longjia Airport

Changchun Longjia International Airport is an important hub for Jilin province and northeast China. It is located in a largely agricultural area between two major cities: about twenty miles east of Changchun and forty-seven miles west of Jilin City. The airport is operated jointly by both cities, which closed their own airports after the new airport opened. The Longjia Airport Station is located underneath the airport and is the only intermediate station between the two cities on the Changji HSR line. The high-speed station allows passengers to travel to the airport from Jilin City in thirty minutes, and from Changchun in just fifteen minutes from centrally located stations in both towns.

9 / 10: Taiwan Taoyuan Airport

Taiwan Taoyuan International Airport is the largest and busiest of five airports in Taiwan. It is located in the developing city of Taoyuan about twenty-five miles southwest of Taipei. The Taoyuan HSR station is located in the center of Taoyuan, not directly at the airport. A bus connects the station to the airport, with future metro connections under construction.

10 / 10: Roma Fiumicino Aeroporto

Rome Fiumicino International Airport is a primary airport hub in Italy, located twenty-two miles southwest of the city center in a nature preserve and adjacent to the small town of Fiumicino. The Fiumicino Airport Station is connected to the terminals via skywalk, and has long provided commuter rail connections to all the major train stations in Rome (Rome Termini, Rome Tiburtina, and Rome Ostiense) as well as other important towns near the city. In 2014 Frecciargento (HSR) service was extended to the airport for two daily departures to destinations such as Venice, Bologna, and Milan. Although the trains currently operate on the conventional network, there are future plans to construct dedicated HSR lines to the airport station. Similar service was recently discontinued at Milano Malpensa International Airport.
