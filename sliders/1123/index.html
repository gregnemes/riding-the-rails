Platforms are constructed to accommodate passengers along the length of the train. Some are elevated to the height of the cars while lower platforms require small stairs to board the train. Platform materials may be of concrete or wood plank.
Station platforms provide shelters for passengers awaiting the train. Small shelters may also be used at flag stops with low passenger volume where a station does not exist. Source: W. Berg.
A typical flag depot includes just the essentials: a ticket booth, a waiting room, and a small platform. Source: W. Berg.
At times a flag depot might also accommodate the agent's family within the station. Source: W. Berg.
Combination depots are found in smaller towns where traffic doesn't warrant separate freight and passenger facilities.
A typical passenger station in the author's hometown of Dowagiac, MI. Source: Author.
Most railroads adopted standards for the construction of passenger stations, with elaborate stations being reserved for large cities hosting considerable traffic. Source: W. Berg.
