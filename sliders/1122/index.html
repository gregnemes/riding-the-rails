It was important for employees to be on hand near the tracks for quick or emergency repairs. Railroads provided housing in the form of Section Houses.
The railroad would also construct dwelling houses for other employees, especially in sparsely populated areas.
Most railroads constructed rooming houses at major stations to house train crews and other employees. In some cases other rooming houses such as the YMCA served this function.
The YMCA in Turtle Bay, Manhattan is title "The Railroad Branch" because it catered to railroad crews.
